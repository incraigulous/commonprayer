# Daily Office

The Daily Office is the ancient Christian practice of fixed-hour prayer — praying at set times throughout the day using structured liturgy. The 1979 Book of Common Prayer provides four offices:

| Office | Typical Time | Route |
|---|---|---|
| Morning Prayer | Dawn–mid-morning | `/morning` |
| Noonday Prayer | Around noon | `/noon` |
| Evening Prayer | Late afternoon–evening | `/evening` |
| Compline | Before sleep | `/more` → Compline |

The app auto-selects the appropriate tab when you open it based on the current time:

| Hour | Default office |
|---|---|
| Before 9 am | Morning Prayer |
| 9 am – 1 pm | Noonday Prayer |
| 1 pm – 8 pm | Evening Prayer |
| After 8 pm | Compline |

## What each office contains

Every office is assembled dynamically for today's date and your version setting. The structure follows the BCP:

1. **Opening sentence** — seasonal, drawn from the BCP calendar
2. **Confession of Sin** (Morning and Evening Prayer)
3. **Invitatory and Psalter** — psalms from the 30-day BCP cycle or lectionary override
4. **Scripture readings** — from the Daily Office Lectionary (Year One or Year Two)
5. **Canticles** — between and after readings
6. **Apostles' Creed** (Morning and Evening Prayer)
7. **Prayers** — Lord's Prayer, suffrages, collect of the day, proper collect, and optional prayers
8. **Prayers & Thanksgivings** — including any items from your [Prayer List](prayer-list.md)
9. **Dismissal**

## Meditate timer

Wherever a rubric instructs a period of silence or meditation, a **Meditate** button appears inline. Tap it to open a configurable countdown timer (1–20 minutes, default 5). A bell sounds when the time is complete.

## Version switching

If you have multiple versions configured, inline pill tabs let you switch between Rite II and Rite I (or EOW) at the section level without leaving the office.
