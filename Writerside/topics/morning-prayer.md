# Morning Prayer

Morning Prayer (Mattins) is the first Daily Office of the day. The BCP 1979 provides it in Rite I (traditional language) and Rite II (contemporary language).

## Structure

1. Opening Sentences (seasonal)
2. Confession of Sin
3. Invitatory — *Venite* or *Jubilate*, with antiphon
4. Psalter — psalms for the morning according to the 30-day cycle
5. First Lesson (Old Testament)
6. Canticle — *Te Deum* or *Benedictus es, Domine* (Rite II) / *Benedicite* (seasonal)
7. Second Lesson (New Testament)
8. Canticle — *Benedictus* (Rite II) or *Jubilate*
9. Apostles' Creed
10. Prayers — Lord's Prayer, suffrages, collect of the day
11. Prayers of the People
12. Dismissal

## Gloria Patri

If **Gloria Patri after each Psalm** is enabled in [Settings](settings.md), the doxology "Glory to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit…" is appended after each psalm and canticle.

## Rite I vs. Rite II

Rite I uses the traditional Tudor-era language of the prayer book ("O Lord, open thou our lips"). Rite II uses contemporary language ("Lord, open our lips"). The content is liturgically equivalent; the language differs throughout.

Switch between them using the inline version tabs or change your default in [Settings](settings.md).
